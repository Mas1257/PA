# PA Manifesto

## We build with purpose.

PA is not a collection of features.

Every capability exists because it solves a real problem.

If a feature cannot justify its existence, it does not belong here.

---

## We respect the user's time.

Time is the most valuable resource.

Every unnecessary click,
every repeated action,
every hidden piece of information,
every interruption...

is a cost paid by the user.

PA exists to reduce that cost.

---

## We design for clarity.

Power should never require complexity.

A tool should feel simple,
even when the work behind it is not.

The best interface is one that lets people focus on their work,
not on learning the software.

---

## We never assume.

Questions come before solutions.

Observation comes before implementation.

Understanding comes before optimization.

Good software is built on evidence,
not assumptions.

---

## We think long-term.

Shortcuts become technical debt.

Every decision should still make sense years from now.

Maintainability is a feature.

Consistency is a feature.

Reliability is a feature.

---

## We value quality over quantity.

More features do not create a better product.

Better decisions do.

Adding something is easy.

Maintaining it for years is the real challenge.

---

## We organize knowledge.

Information should be easy to discover.

People should solve problems,
not waste time searching for resources.

Good organization is invisible,
yet invaluable.

---

## We build for people.

Technology should adapt to humans.

Humans should not adapt to technology.

Software should remove friction,
not create it.

---

## Every detail matters.

Small improvements repeated every day
become meaningful over time.

The goal is not to impress users.

The goal is to quietly make their work better.

---

## The measure of success

If PA helps someone finish their work
a little faster,

learn something
a little easier,

or end the day
a little less frustrated,

then it has achieved its purpose.

---

> **Build something worth maintaining.**
